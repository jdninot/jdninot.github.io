About me

“The journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step” — Lao Tzu

I am a statistician from the National University of Colombia with solid theoretical and practical knowledge in the field of statistical-mathematical modelling for data collection, cleaning, analysis, interpretation and presentation. Currently, I am a data analyst at the Laboratory of Innovation, Creativity and New Technologies of the National University of Colombia (LAB101).

My main research interest is the study of the brain through mathematics, statistics, and computer science with the aim of discovering the patterns that underlie the dynamics of thoughts, memory, consciousness and dreams. However, I am also interested in the study of the environment to contribute to research on the effects of climate change on planet earth and its mitigation.

Research areas: Statistical machine learning, stochastic processes, bayesian statistics, generalized linear models, functional analysis, spatial and temporal statistics, applied to artificial intelligence, neurosciences, bioinformatics, public health, environmental and economic studies.
